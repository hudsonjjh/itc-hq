·How This Works›

Research deep. Audit cold. Script from LOCKED only.

The three design laws
Law 1

Every step is portable

Each step's output is a complete standalone deliverable that carries everything the next step needs. Assume the next step runs in a different chat, in a different AI, with zero memory of this one. If an output cannot be pasted cold into a fresh chat and still work, the step was run wrong.

Law 2

Research depth is the foundation

Steps 1 to 3 build a reference document, not a summary. A Short and a long form both come out of the same research body, so research is never scoped to runtime. Research first, decide format later.

Law 3

Nothing is fact unless it is LOCKED

LOCKED = confirmed by a primary or factory grade source. LIKELY = strong evidence, single or secondary source. UNVERIFIED = lore, forum only, or unconfirmed. Only LOCKED goes in a script or on a card. Every claim in every deliverable carries a tier and a named source.

Source hierarchy

Factory brochures, OEM service and owner manuals, and material from Ford, GM, Cummins, International, Navistar, Bosch, BorgWarner, and Dana outrank everything. Credible archives second. Forums are leads, never proof.

Style rules

No em dashes in any script or card copy. No emojis. Ranges written as "1994 to 1997". Voice: direct, mechanical, short sentences, a little personality, no hype, no filler. Sign off: "Inside The Case." or "Thanks for watching, Inside The Case."

Prompt 1 · Deep research
Inside The Case deep research pass. You are building a reference
document, book report style, not a summary and not a script. It will
be handed to other people and other AI models that have no access to
this conversation, so it must stand completely on its own.

TOPIC: [FILL IN. the exact subject. e.g. BW4407 transfer case]
DIRECTION: [FILL IN. any angle, constraints, or known starting points, or "none"]

Use web search extensively throughout. Search in many passes with
varied terms: factory literature, service manuals, parts catalogs,
supplier documentation, credible archives. Depth over polish. Do not
scope the research to a Short or a long video. Research the subject
completely; format gets decided later.

Work through, at minimum:
- Full production history and timeline, year by year where the record
  supports it
- Every engineering specification available: displacement, bore and
  stroke, compression, output figures by year and application, block
  and head casting details, injection or fuel system, turbo or
  induction, cooling, oiling
- What changed and when, with the specific model year of each change
- Why each change happened and what problem it solved
- Every transmission, transfer case, and axle it was paired with, by
  year and application
- Factory options, packages, and availability, including what was NOT
  available and in which years
- Known failure modes, weak points, and service realities
- What replaced it and what it replaced
- Production numbers, market context, and competitors where documented
- Common misconceptions and internet myths, each addressed directly
  and tiered
- Anything genuinely surprising or under discussed that would make
  good video content

TIERS. Every factual claim carries a tier and a named source, inline,
no exceptions. LOCKED = primary or factory grade source. LIKELY =
strong evidence, single or secondary source. UNVERIFIED = lore, forum
only, or unconfirmed. Where sources disagree, say so, present both,
and tier accordingly. Where something cannot be confirmed, say so
plainly under open questions. Never guess to fill a gap.

SOURCE HIERARCHY. Factory brochures, OEM service and owner manuals,
and material from Ford, GM, Cummins, International, Navistar, Bosch,
BorgWarner, and Dana first. Credible archives second. Forums are
leads, never proof. Name sources precisely: document title and year,
not "various forums". Keep figures exact with units; never round when
the source gives a precise number.

STAY ON SUBJECT. No adjacent history padding, no filler, no general
automotive commentary.

OVER-PRODUCE LEADS. Note every adjacency, variant, and
identity-confusion candidate you notice even if you do not chase it
this pass, and record it in the coverage map below for a later
expansion pass.

OUTPUT, plain text in this exact structure:
1. SUBJECT SUMMARY
2. TIMELINE, year by year
3. SPECIFICATION TABLES
4. SYSTEMS BREAKDOWN, one section per major system
5. DRIVETRAIN PAIRINGS by year and application
6. OPTIONS AND AVAILABILITY, including negative findings
7. FAILURE MODES AND SERVICE NOTES
8. MYTHS AND MISCONCEPTIONS, tiered
9. CONTENT ANGLES worth exploring, flagged but not developed
10. OPEN QUESTIONS and unresolved items
11. SOURCE LIST, named sources with links
12. MASTER CLAIM TABLE: every factual claim in the document with its
    tier and source
13. COVERAGE MAP AND UNCHASED LEADS: the categories fully covered,
    the categories only partially covered, and every adjacency,
    variant, or identity-confusion lead you noticed but did not
    search. This feeds a later expansion pass so it does not repeat
    work.

STYLE. No em dashes. No emojis. Ranges written as "1994 to 1997".

End the response by attaching the complete document as a PDF named
ITC_TOPIC_RESEARCH.pdf (swap TOPIC for a short tag). Keep the full
plain text in the chat as well; the text is what moves between steps.
If the reply runs long, keep going until the document is complete;
if it cuts off, continue when asked.

Prompt 3 · Expansion pass
You are running the Expansion Pass for an Inside The Case Auto
research document. A two-pass research document is below. It has
already been researched once and self-critiqued once in the same
window. Your job is not to redo that work and not to audit it. Your
job is to make the document exhaustive by finding everything it does
not yet contain.

STANDING RULES. This prompt runs cold. Assume no prior context.
- Every factual claim carries a tier. LOCKED means confirmed by a
  primary or factory grade source that actually covers the part,
  application, or service condition. LIKELY means strong but single
  or secondary source, or the source does not fully establish factory
  allocation, an exact breakpoint, or original engineering intent.
  UNVERIFIED means lore, forum only, indirect, conflicting, or
  limited to a search status. UNVERIFIED does not mean false. It
  means it cannot be stated as fact from the recovered record.
- Source hierarchy. Factory brochures, OEM service and owner manuals,
  and material from Ford, GM, Cummins, International, Navistar,
  Bosch, BorgWarner, Dana, ARB, Accuride and equivalent manufacturers
  outrank everything. Credible archives second. Forums are leads,
  never proof. A replacement parts catalog locks service availability
  or interchange only, never factory build combinations. Name the
  source on every claim.
- Search to confirm. Do not reason a fact into existence. Use web
  search. If a thing cannot be confirmed, say so plainly and tier it
  down.
- Stay on subject. Adjacent material is in scope only where it
  changes how the subject is understood, identified, or serviced. Do
  not drift into a different video.
- Style. No em dashes. No emojis. Ranges written as "1994 to 1997".
  Continue the existing C-number claim IDs and S-number source IDs
  from where the attached document ends. Do not renumber existing
  claims.

MANDATE. Work outward from the subject and find the parts of the
story the first two passes did not ask about. Run a distinct
multi-pass search for each category below. For every category, either
add new tiered claims with named sources, or record a negative
finding as UNVERIFIED research status that names the searches you
ran. Do not leave a category blank without stating you searched it.

1. Coverage map first. Read the document and list, in one block,
every question it already answers well. Then list the open edges:
the adjacent systems, years, variants, and identity questions it
touches but does not resolve. Everything below aims at those edges,
not at what is already covered.

2. Adjacency. The neighboring systems and parts that share this
subject's story. The mating transmission, transfer case, or
driveshaft. The front axle or the opposite component. The brake and
wheel-end system as its own subject. The frame, tow rating, or GVWR
context that explains why the part exists. Add only what changes
identification or understanding of the subject.

3. Cross-manufacturer and period context. What the direct
competitors used in the same years for the same job, only where the
contrast explains a Ford or supplier decision or corrects a
comparison viewers commonly make. No competitor deep-dive for its
own sake.

4. Supplier and business history. Why this supplier, why this part,
the corporate versus vendor decision, licensing, plant or country
sourcing, and any named supplier program. This is often where the
strongest reason-why angle lives.

5. Running changes, variants, and special builds. Mid-year and
running production changes with dates where documented. Regional or
export variants. Fleet, government, police, ambulance, or DSO
special builds. These are frequent identity traps and strong hook
material.

6. Interchange and the swap and rebuild ecosystem. What actually
interchanges, what does not, the common upgrades and their gotchas,
and the minimum information needed to order the right parts. High
value for a technical audience and a strong retention layer.

7. Identity and naming confusion. Hunt every place the subject is
mislabeled, shares a name with a different part, or is confused with
a neighbor. List each confusion as its own tiered entry with the
correction and its source. This is a priority category.
Identity-confusion and misconception corrections are the channel's
highest-reach angle type, so mine this surface hard.

8. Misconceptions and lore, tiered. Extend the myth section. For
each item give a verdict of CONFIRMED, CONTRADICTED, HALF TRUE, or
NOT PROVEN, with the source and tier. Trace lore to its origin where
you can find it.

9. Surprises and counterintuitive findings. Anything that
contradicts what a knowledgeable viewer would assume. Any
dated-change trap. Any place where the obvious answer is wrong. Flag
each as a candidate hook.

10. Negative space, done rigorously. For every "was X available"
question the subject invites, either lock the answer or state
exactly what a document would need to say to lock it, and mark the
current finding UNVERIFIED. Do not turn a failed search into a
locked absence. One brochure or one owner guide cannot prove
universal absence across a whole model range.

OUTPUT. Restate the complete document with your additions folded in,
in the same section structure and tier discipline as the version
below, so the result is one standalone expanded research document.
Continue the C-number and S-number sequences without gaps. Then
append two short blocks:

EXPANSION LOG. What you added by category, and what you searched but
did not find.

HANDOFF TO AUDIT. The new claims most in need of hostile checking
and why. The next step is an independent audit run in a fresh window
with no memory of this chat, so the expanded document must stand
entirely on its own.

End the response by attaching the expanded document as a PDF named
ITC_TOPIC_RESEARCH_V3.pdf. Keep the full plain text in the chat as
well; the text is what moves to the audit.

Legend · every card type & option

Card types · the "type:" line

spec
Label + value grid. Rows read LABEL | value. Bullet dot on each. Values wrap, take *accent* coloring, and can stack > sub-lines. Auto-shrinks to fit.
timeline
Year + event. Rows read YEAR | event. Dots down a connecting line. Auto-shrinks to fit.
table
Multi-column grid. First row is the header. Rows read COL | COL | COL. The third column auto-colors in the accent. Does not shrink, so it warns if content overflows.
compare
Two panel versus. Rows read LABEL | left | right. The first row names the two panels, e.g. SPEC | CHEVY 350 | FORD 351W. Center divider, VS mark, auto-shrinks to fit.
