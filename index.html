·The system›

One topic in. One finished Short out.

One session, one Short, start to finish. Pick the exact topic, research just that topic with web search, audit the facts cold in a fresh chat, then script and build from LOCKED facts only. No warehouse of facts to maintain, no picking scripts out of a pile. Everything runs on Claude: one Project, one model, two kinds of chat.

The media

·The five rules›
Accuracy first
Never guess on years, horsepower, torque, injection, pump type, turbo, or factory options. If it is not confirmed, it stays off the cards.
Every fact tiered
Locked = confirmed by a factory or OEM grade source. Likely = strong but single or secondary source. Unverified = lore, forum only, or could not confirm.
Locked only
Only Locked facts get stated as fact in scripts and put on cards. Likely and Unverified stay off screen. They can live in a pinned comment, never on a card.
Source order
Factory brochures and OEM or owner manuals first. Credible archives second. Forums last, as a lead only, never as proof.
House style
No em dashes in any script or card copy. Visual styling elements like timeline connectors are the only exception. No emojis. Write ranges as "1994 to 1997". Never skip the audit pass on a fact sheet.

You are auditing a fact sheet for one automotive YouTube Short, from a
channel that lives on accuracy. You had no part in building it. Do not
rewrite it beyond what fails.

Verify against sources, not memory. Use web search to check any LOCKED
fact you cannot independently confirm from a primary or factory grade

For each LOCKED fact, verify it deserves LOCKED. Report ONLY:
- facts that are wrong,
- facts tiered too high,
- anything important that is missing.

Inside The Case Short package. Build everything from the audited fact
sheet below. Use LOCKED facts only. LIKELY and UNVERIFIED stay off the
script and off the cards.

TOPIC: [FILL IN. the exact part or comparison. e.g. BW4407 transfer case]
ANGLE: [FILL IN. the one line hook]
FACT SHEET: [PASTE THE AUDITED FACT SHEET FROM STEP 2]

OUTPUT, IN THIS ORDER:

1. SCRIPT. Inside The Case voice: direct, mechanical, short sentences,
a little personality, no hype, no filler. Structure: hook, setup,
mechanical truth, one strong retention beat, payoff, sign-off
("Inside The Case." or "Thanks for watching, Inside The Case.").

2. NEW CLAIMS. List every factual claim in the script that is NOT in

3. CARD SPECS. 3 to 5 vertical cards written directly in the Card

footer out, the generator fills them in.

4. COMMENT VULNERABILITIES. What a knowledgeable commenter could
dispute, so it can be pinned, changed, or left alone on purpose.

STYLE: no em dashes and no en dashes anywhere in script or card
copy. No emojis. Ranges written as "1994 to 1997".

04The QC pass15 to 20 minutes›
One session · one Short

This replaces the old correction slog. It is short because the facts were verified upstream, so you are checking two things, not auditing a whole script.

  • Read NEW CLAIMS carefully. This is the only place fresh, unaudited facts can enter. Anything Unverified gets cut from the script or confirmed by you, with a source you actually checked, before it stays.
  • Skim the script for voice and flow. Read it out loud once. If a line does not sound like the channel, fix the line, not the system.
  • Glance at COMMENT VULNERABILITIES. Decide now: pin a comment, change a line, or leave it on purpose.
DELIVERABLE 1 DONE · The script is approved and saved.
05Record the voiceoverNo AI, just you›
One session · one Short

Read the approved script out loud and record it. Clean audio drops straight into the edit.

  • Read it aloud once or twice first to get comfortable and catch any tongue-twister lines.
  • Kill the noise. Quiet room, fan and AC off, windows closed, no TV. Background hum is the one thing that cannot be fixed in the edit.
  • Get close to the mic. Phone Voice Memos or Recorder app about a hand's width from your mouth, or a USB mic if you have one. Avoid speakerphone and the laptop's built in mic.
  • Match the voice. Direct and steady, like you are explaining it to someone who knows trucks. Not hype, not radio DJ.
  • Pace it. Clear and even, with a small pause at the end of each sentence. Easy silence is easy to trim.
  • Flubbed a line? Do not restart the whole thing. Pause, then read that one line again. The bad take gets cut.
  • Do two or three full takes if you can, so there are options.
  • Listen back once. Every word audible, no big background noise? Good. Save as mp3, m4a, or wav.

ROLE
You are the social packaging partner for Inside The Case Auto, an
automotive channel that lives on accuracy. I will give you the finished
script and its package notes for one Short. Turn them into a copy ready
package for a YouTube Short and a Facebook or Instagram Reel. Do not
rewrite the script.

SCRIPT: [PASTE THE FINISHED SCRIPT]
NOTES: [PASTE NEW CLAIMS AND COMMENT VULNERABILITIES FROM THE PACKAGE]

BUILD ALL OF THIS, COPY READY, IN THIS ORDER

1. YOUTUBE TITLES. 10 options, no hashtags. Curiosity without dishonest
clickbait, accurate to what the video shows. Recommend one, one line why.

2. YOUTUBE DESCRIPTIONS. 2 options. Each opens with two or three lines
on what the video covers, channel voice, short sentences. Each ends
with the CORRECTIONS block below.

3. CORRECTIONS AND CLARIFICATIONS (inside each description). Anywhere
the script simplified, rounded, or repeated a common belief the notes

6. FACEBOOK OR INSTAGRAM REEL CAPTION. Slightly more casual. Hashtags
fine. Fold the single most important correction into the caption in
plain language.

7. PINNED COMMENT. Preempt the predictable technical objection. Calm
and factual, never combative. If a challenger is vague, the burden of

STYLE
No em dashes. No emojis. Write ranges as "1994 to 1997". Label every

Legend · every card type & option

Card types · the "type:" line

spec
Label + value grid. Rows read LABEL | value. Bullet dot on each. Values wrap, take *accent* coloring, and can stack > sub-lines. Auto-shrinks to fit.
timeline
Year + event. Rows read YEAR | event. Dots down a connecting line. Auto-shrinks to fit.
table
Multi-column grid. First row is the header. Rows read COL | COL | COL. The third column auto-colors in the accent. Does not shrink, so it warns if content overflows.
